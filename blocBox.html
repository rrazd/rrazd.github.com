BlocBox SAAS Web Application UX and UI

Background

BlocBox was a project that I participated in as part of my appreticeship at Bloc.io. Blocbox is a SaaS (Software as a Service) web application which allows people to collect information and store it in a single place. A person can create simple documents, store images, links to websites, and collaborate with others. The web application had had all its business requirements and specifictions listed, the task at hand was to design BlocBox.

Competitive Analysis and Research

The first phase of the project was to conduct competitive analysis and research. Competitive analysis included SWOT analysis: Conducting surveys, interviewing potential users, developing personas and writing user stories helped plan and define the strategy for designing this web application.
The survey was created using Google Forms and was based on the following points of interest:

  • How our users interact with their devices

  • What they look at online

  • If they save content

  • If they write and save notes to remember things later

  • About the tools they currently use their profession

  • If they collaborate and share resources with their co-workers

  • If they use social network logins to sign up for websites their age and where they live

The individuals polled consisted of many types of possible users from designers to tech professionals. Testing

Usability testing was performed using Usability Hub, in particular the Five Second Test and Nav Flow Test were conducted.
There were 30 reponses for the Five Second Test, the results showed that users always recognized and remembered the name and logo of Blocbox. Most agreed that the design looked professional and clean.
For the Nav Flow Test, the question posed was: "How would you upload an image to your dashboard?" Based on 10 responses, the success rate was 66%, with a completion time of 18 seconds. This was lower than expected and indicated further improvement was needed.

Conclusion

InVision was used to create a clickable prototype of the final designs that was showcased to the client. Throughout the research, prototyping and design phases the client was kept up to date with the progress, this ensured everyone was on the same page.
